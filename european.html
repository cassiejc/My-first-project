Bibury

Welcome to Bibury! A minuscule civil parish, perhaps the tiniest in this selection. But it’s also home to the emblematic Arlington Row situated on River Coln, a tributary of the Thames. It was first established in 1380 (!) with monastic wool stores. The steeply vaulted homes were converted into weaver’s cottages in the 17th century in order to supply cloth to the nearby mill, back when the wool trade with the new world started picking up and accounted for almost half of England’s economy. Arlington Row is a national conservation area and is depicted on the inside cover of all United Kingdom passports.

Asymmetric personality that incorporates symmetry

When I was talking about interiors with people from other countries, there was a time when the discussion extended to the architectural styles, artistry, and aesthetics of each country. Among them, although in other countries symmetrical symmetry is considered beautiful, It turned out that Japan is the only culture that sees asymmetrical asymmetry as beautiful. The Japanese, who chose the way of coexisting with nature, I think I was looking at the asymmetry of "wabi (wabi), sabi (sabi)". It's hard to say that they have vast lands like in foreign countries, but there were some predecessors who owned large tracts of land. However, they did not build their buildings symmetrically, and neither the interior design nor the garden design is symmetrical.

If anything, it's a style that connects our lives with the beauty of nature. I think you can catch a glimpse of the aesthetic sense even if you take a single tea set. The rugged texture, the distorted shape, and the different colors depending on the part of the tea utensil, They have the sensibility to find beauty. While accepting and snuggling with things that change and things that are different, We can create something that can be said to be the only culture in the world, Because we have such sensibilities, In a sense, it may be natural to adopt Western symmetrical symmetry.

Blaise Hamlet

Blaise Hamlet adalah sebuah pemukiman (hamlet) yang terletak di Hallen Road, wilayah barat-laut Bristol. Pemukiman tersebut terdiri dari 9 pondok kecil yang dikelilingi oleh taman hijau. Kompleks pondok tersebut dilengkapi dengan sebuah sundial, yaitu alat penunjuk waktu berdasarkan bayangan matahari. Blaise Hamlet pertama kali dibangun pada tahun 1811 oleh John Scandret Harford, yang juga pemilik Blaise Castle. Pondok-pondok tersebut memiliki bentuk yang unik dan dilengkapi dengan cerobong asap, beberapa di antaranya masih menggunakan atap jerami. Semua pondok tersebut juga memiliki nama masing-masing yaitu Circular Cottage, Dial Cottage, Diamond Cottage, Double Cottage, Dutch Cottage, Oak Cottage, Rose Cottage, Sweetbriar Cottage, dan Vine Cottage. Dari 9 pondok tersebut, semuanya merupakan milik perseorangan, dan hanya Rose Cottage yang bisa disewa oleh publik.
